LIBS (Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy) provides rapid elemental analysis but is not equivalent to certified laboratory analysis. No certified assay currently exists for lithium from this operation.

Battery-grade qualification requires laboratory analysis with ICP-OES or ICP-MS from an accredited facility. Lithium pricing varies dramatically by end-use — ceramic-grade vs. battery-grade commands vastly different prices.

The $400/t seller price vs. the $10,000–15,000/t battery-grade reference tells the full story.
The $400/t seller indication is consistent with low-grade spodumene concentrate or petalite — material unsuitable for battery applications without significant upgrading. The $10,000–15,000/t battery-grade reference applies only to battery-grade lithium carbonate or hydroxide with >99.5% purity. Li₂O 6.40% is a modest grade for hard-rock lithium. Albemarle's Spodumene concentrate is typically 5.5–7% Li₂O and sold at ~$600–900/t. The price gap to battery-grade is enormous and requires a conversion refinery — not currently available in the DRC.
Lithium needs a certified assay before serious buyer conversations.
Laboratory ICP analysis from an accredited facility (SGS, ALS, Bureau Veritas) will determine whether this material is suitable for ceramic, industrial, or battery applications.
