Market Context — Q2 2026

Global tin supply deficit creates opportunity for new non-Chinese sources.

Supply Crisis

China controls ~45% of global tin smelting. Indonesia's export licensing delays and Myanmar mine closures have tightened global supply. Non-Chinese tin sources command premium pricing from European and North American end-users seeking supply chain diversification.

Demand Growth

Tin is critical for lead-free soldering in electronics, EV manufacturing, AI hardware, and photovoltaic ribbon. Global tin demand is projected to grow as the energy transition accelerates — particularly in solar panel manufacturing and EV battery systems.

Critical Mineral Status

Tin is classified as a critical and strategic mineral by the EU, USA, and Japan. The EU Critical Raw Materials Act sets targets for domestic extraction and processing — making new non-Chinese cassiterite sources geopolitically significant.

End-Use Applications

Electronics & Soldering

Lead-free solder alloys for PCB assembly, semiconductor packaging, and consumer electronics manufacturing.

Tin Plating

Corrosion-resistant coatings for steel food cans, electrical connectors, and automotive components.

Photovoltaic Ribbon

Tin-lead solder ribbon for solar cell interconnection — a growing segment in renewable energy manufacturing.

Chemicals & Alloys

Tin chemicals for PVC stabilizers, biocides, and bronze/babbitt alloys for bearings and industrial applications.
