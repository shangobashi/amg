Market Context — Q2 2026

Strategic mineral demand surging on 5G, AI, and defense technology.

Supply Crisis

China controls ~45% of global tin smelting. Indonesia's export licensing delays and Myanmar mine closures have tightened global supply. Non-Chinese tin sources command premium pricing from European and North American end-users seeking supply chain diversification.

Demand Growth

Tin is critical for lead-free soldering in electronics, EV manufacturing, AI hardware, and photovoltaic ribbon. Global tin demand is projected to grow as the energy transition accelerates — particularly in solar panel manufacturing and EV battery systems.

Critical Mineral Status

Tin is classified as a critical and strategic mineral by the EU, USA, and Japan. The EU Critical Raw Materials Act sets targets for domestic extraction and processing — making new non-Chinese coltan sources geopolitically significant.

End-Use Applications

Capacitors

Tantalum capacitors are essential for mobile devices, 5G base stations, automotive electronics, and aerospace systems due to high capacitance in small size.

5G Infrastructure

5G networks require high-performance capacitors for base station power management and signal processing — driving tantalum demand.

Defense & Aerospace

Tantalum-strengthened superalloys for land-based gas turbines and industrial process equipment operated at high temperature.

Superalloys

Tantalum alloys used in jet engine components, missile guidance systems, and armor due to high melting point and corrosion resistance.
